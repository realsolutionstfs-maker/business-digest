Decisions are slow. Everyone agrees on the problem but not the solution. This is a common bottleneck. The tool: The "Pre-Mortem" exercise. It's a proactive risk assessment. How it works: Imagine your project has failed spectacularly. Now, work backward. What went wrong? List every conceivable reason for failure. This shifts perspective from optimism to critical thinking. It uncovers hidden risks before they materialize. Example: A startup is launching a new subscription service. Instead of just planning success, they conduct a pre-mortem. They imagine the service failed after six months. Reasons identified: high churn due to poor onboarding, unexpected competitor launch, payment processing issues, marketing message confusion. They then build mitigation strategies for each. How to apply: Before your next major project or decision, gather your team. Dedicate 60 minutes. Ask: "It's six months from now, and this project has been a disaster. What happened?"
