[TIMECODE 0:30-3:00] — ACT I: THE RISE
Chesky and Gebbia moved to San Francisco in 2007. A design conference was coming to town. Hotels were sold out.

They bought three air mattresses. Called it "Air Bed and Breakfast." Charged $80 per person per night.

[SOURCE: Wikipedia]

[TIMECODE 3:00-7:00] — ACT II: THE STRUGGLE
Year one: zero revenue. Investors rejected them 7 times. Paul Graham of Y Combinator initially passed.

[SOURCE: Founder interviews]

They created Obama O's cereal — $40 per box — to fund the company during the 2008 election. Sold 800 boxes. Raised $30,000.

[PAUSE — 1s]

Then in 2009, a crisis: a host's apartment was trashed by a guest. No insurance. No recourse.

This became the defining moment. They created the $1 million host guarantee. Built the trust that made the platform possible.

[TIMECODE 7:00-9:30] — ACT III: THE RESOLUTION
By 2020, Airbnb was valued at $100 billion. 4 million hosts. 800 million guests.

But in March 2020, bookings dropped 90% in 8 weeks. The company laid off 25% of staff. IPO was delayed.

[SOURCE: The Airbnb Story]

The company survived. Went public in December 2020 at $47 billion.

[TIMECODE 9:30-10:00] — TAKEAWAY
The lesson: survive long enough to get lucky. [EMPHASIS: Survive long enough to get lucky.] Airbnb almost died 8 times. Each time they found a creative way to stay alive.
