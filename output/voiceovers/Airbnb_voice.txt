They bought three air mattresses. Called it Air Bed and Breakfast. Charged 80 dollars per person per night.

Year one: zero revenue. Investors rejected them seven times. Paul Graham of Y Combinator initially passed.

So they created Obama O's cereal. Forty dollars per box. Sold 800 boxes. Raised 30,000 dollars to fund the company during the 2008 election.

This became the defining moment. They created the one million dollar host guarantee. Built the trust that made the platform possible.

By 2020, Airbnb was valued at 100 billion dollars. Four million hosts. Eight hundred million guests.

But in March 2020, bookings dropped 90 percent in eight weeks. The company laid off 25 percent of staff. The IPO was delayed.

Airbnb survived. Went public in December 2020 at 47 billion dollars.

The lesson: survive long enough to get lucky. Airbnb almost died eight times. Each time they found a creative way to stay alive.
